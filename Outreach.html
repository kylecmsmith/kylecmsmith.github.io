Outreach

Speaking in classrooms

Through the Skype a Scientist program, I have spoken in various K-12 classrooms about dogs, wolves, primates, and animal behavior in general. I am available to speak to classrooms around the world in English or Spanish. If you would like me to speak in your classroom, please contact me.

I can also speak in person at classrooms in the State College, PA, area. This can include giving live, interactive demonstrations of dog cognition where students can participate in the experiments themselves. Please contact me if interested.

Stories Under the Sky

Along with Abby Gancz, I co-organized the Stories Under the Sky four-part event series at the Penn State Arboretum, funded by the Tygel Endowment. We presented about the significance of the night sky in cultures around the world, including how the sky is linked to cosmological beliefs, how uncommon astronomical events are interpreted and how the sky is used for timekeeping and navigation. Then, a guest speaker told sky-related stories from their own culture (we had Islamic, Chinese, Jewish, and Nigerian sky stories). This was followed by stargazing through a telescope. We plan to continue this event series in the future at both the Arboretum and other locations. Please contact me if interested.
