Outreach

Speaking in classrooms

Through the Skype a Scientist program, I have spoken in various K-12 classrooms about dogs, wolves, primates, and animal behavior in general. I am available to speak to classrooms around the world in English or Spanish. If you would like me to speak in your classroom, please contact me.

I can also speak in person at classrooms in the State College, PA, area. This can include giving live, interactive demonstrations of dog cognition where students can participate in the experiments themselves. Please contact me if interested.

Stories Under the Sky

Along with Abby Gancz, I am co-organizing the upcoming Stories Under the Sky event at the Penn State Arboretum, funded by the Tygel Endowment. Members of the Anthropology department will present about the significance of the night sky in cultures around the world. Then, a guest speaker will tell a star-related story from their own culture. This will be followed by optional stargazing. There will be four sessions, and registration is required in advance for each:

Thu., Aug. 5, 2021 at 7 PM: Cosmological beliefs and an Islamic star story

Thu., Aug. 12, 2021 at 7 PM: Uncommon astronomical events and a Chinese star story

Thu., Aug. 26, 2021 at 7 PM: Keeping track of time and a Jewish star story

Thu., Sep. 2, 2021 at 7 PM: Celestial navigation and a Nigerian star story
